# AI‑Native UX Lab
Independent research initiative exploring what user interfaces become when AI is no longer an add‑on but the primary computational substrate. We investigate fluid, generative, and emotion‑aware interfaces built from semantic surfaces rather than static applications, using research‑driven prototyping as our core method. The manifesto captures our evolving thinking at the intersection of generative UI, human‑AI rapport, and systems‑level interface design, as we work toward a new paradigm for AI‑native interaction.

# Manifesto

## Premise
Artificial intelligence is no longer a feature layered onto software systems; it is becoming the primary computational substrate. This shift demands a rethinking of user interfaces not as static artifacts, but as adaptive, generative systems that mediate intent, context, and meaning in real time.

## The Problem with App‑Centric UI
Application‑centric interfaces assume fixed workflows, predefined tools, and stable user intent. As AI systems become continuous, contextual, and generative, these assumptions break down, leading to friction, cognitive overload, and brittle interaction models.

## AI as the Primary Substrate
When AI becomes the underlying medium of interaction, interfaces are no longer driven by screens and controls, but by reasoning, interpretation, and synthesis. Interaction shifts from commanding tools to collaborating with systems that actively participate in sense‑making.

## From Applications to Semantic Surfaces
We propose semantic surfaces as the fundamental unit of interaction: dynamic, meaning‑oriented interface constructs that emerge in response to user intent and system understanding, rather than being predefined by application boundaries.

## Fluid Interaction and Generative UI
Generative UI enables interfaces to be composed, adapted, and dissolved dynamically. Interaction becomes fluid, allowing the system to reshape representation and control structures as goals, context, and understanding evolve.

## Human‑AI Rapport
Effective AI‑native interfaces require more than functional correctness. Emotion‑aware interaction, trust calibration, and relational continuity are essential for building rapport between humans and AI systems that operate over extended periods and complex tasks.

## Systems‑Level Interface Design
User interfaces must be understood as systems composed of interacting layers: reasoning, representation, affect, and control. Designing at this level shifts focus from visual layout to the orchestration of cognitive and emotional dynamics.

## Research‑Driven Prototyping
Prototypes are treated as research instruments rather than products. Through iterative experimentation, we explore how theoretical concepts manifest in practice, using prototypes to refine both design principles and underlying assumptions.

## Implications and Open Questions
This paradigm raises fundamental questions about evaluation, authorship, agency, and responsibility in AI‑mediated interaction. Many of these questions remain open and are central to ongoing research.

## Direction
AI‑Native UX Lab is an ongoing exploration of AI‑native interaction paradigms. Our work prioritizes conceptual clarity, empirical experimentation, and openness, with the goal of contributing to a deeper understanding of how humans and intelligent systems can collaborate through fluid, generative interfaces.
